## 🇭🇰 Hong Kong Talent Admission Schemes

---

### 1. Top Talent Pass Scheme (TTPS) 高端人才通行證計劃
Launched in 2022 to attract high-calibre global professionals. No job offer required.

**Three categories:**

- **Category A** — Annual income of **HKD 2.5 million+** in the preceding year. Initial visa validity is **3 years** (extended from 2 years as of October 2024).
- **Category B** — Bachelor's degree from one of **198 eligible top universities**, with **3+ years** of work experience.
- **Category C** — Bachelor's degree from the same eligible universities, with **less than 3 years** of experience (subject to an annual quota).

As of November 2024, 13 additional top Mainland and overseas universities were added, bringing the total to 198 eligible institutions.

---

### 2. Quality Migrant Admission Scheme (QMAS) 優秀人才入境計劃
A points-based scheme for highly skilled individuals **without a prior job offer**. No sector restriction.

**Two assessment routes:**
- **General Points Test (GPT)** — scored across: Age, Academic Qualifications, Language Proficiency, Work Experience, Annual Income, and Business Ownership.
- **Achievement-based Points Test (APT)** — for individuals with outstanding achievements (e.g. Olympic medals, Nobel Prize, major international awards).

The updated **Talent List** (effective March 2025) covers **60 professions across 9 industry segments**; professionals on this list enjoy facilitated processing under QMAS. Initial stay is 36 months.

---

### 3. General Employment Policy (GEP) 一般就業政策
The standard work visa route for professionals with a **confirmed job offer** from a Hong Kong employer.

Key requirements include a good education background, technical qualifications or proven professional experience, and being in a position to make a substantial contribution to the Hong Kong economy.

Employers hiring professionals listed on the Talent List no longer need to prove local recruitment difficulties, expediting the hiring process. Initial stay is 36 months, typically renewed on a 3+2 year pattern.

---

### 4. Admission Scheme for Mainland Talents & Professionals (ASMTP) 專才計劃
Equivalent to the GEP but specifically for **Mainland Chinese** applicants with a confirmed job offer. Same Talent List exemption from market availability testing applies.

---

### 5. Technology Talent Admission Scheme (TechTAS) 科技人才入境計劃
A fast-track scheme for technology professionals sponsored by eligible tech companies.

Companies and applicants may now file quota requests and visa applications **in parallel**, cutting processing time by up to 4 weeks. The previous requirement to be engaged specifically in one of 14 designated technology areas has also been lifted, opening the scheme to a wider range of specialists.

Key requirements: STEM degree from a recognised university, employer must hold a valid quota from the Innovation and Technology Commission (ITC), and remuneration must meet prevailing market levels. Initial visa is 24 months.

---

### 6. Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates (IANG) 非本地畢業生留港計劃
For students who have **graduated from a Hong Kong university** — allows them to stay or return to work without a prior job offer. Initial 12-month stay to seek employment.

## 🇭🇰 Hong Kong Talent Admission Schemes
*Source: [HKSAR Immigration Department](https://www.immd.gov.hk/eng/services/index.html#tab_b_1)*

---

### 1. Top Talent Pass Scheme (TTPS) 高端人才通行證計劃

Targets high-income professionals and graduates of top global universities. No job offer is required upon application. Three categories:

**Category A** — Annual taxable income of **HKD 2.5 million or above** in the preceding year. Initial stay: **36 months.**

**Category B** — Degree from an eligible university + **at least 3 years of work experience** in the past 5 years. Initial stay: **24 months.**

**Category C** — Degree from an eligible university obtained **within the past 5 years**, with less than 3 years of work experience. Subject to an annual quota on a first-come, first-served basis. Initial stay: **24 months.**

The eligible universities list is based on the top 100 in four major global rankings (Times Higher Education, QS, U.S. News, and Shanghai Jiao Tong), plus specialist lists for hospitality, top Mainland Chinese universities, and Art & Design.

---

### 2. General Employment Policy (GEP) 一般就業政策
*(For overseas, Taiwan and Macao residents)*

The standard employment-tied scheme. A confirmed job offer is required. Key requirements: the applicant must have secured a job relevant to their academic qualifications or work experience that cannot be readily filled by the local workforce; the remuneration package must be at the prevailing market rate; and the applicant must have a good education background, technical qualifications, or proven professional experience.

Employers are not required to prove local recruitment difficulties if the post falls under the Talent List professions, or if the role offers an annual salary of HKD 2 million or above. Stay pattern: **3+3+2 years.**

---

### 3. Admission Scheme for Mainland Talents and Professionals (ASMTP) 專才計劃
*(For Mainland China residents only)*

The Mainland equivalent of the GEP, with no sector restriction. Key requirements are identical: a relevant job secured that cannot be readily filled locally, remuneration at the prevailing market rate, and a good education background or proven professional experience. The same Talent List exemption from local recruitment proof applies, and the stay pattern is also 3+3+2 years.

---

### 4. GEP / ASMTP — Technical Professionals Stream (TP Stream) 🆕

A newly introduced stream under both GEP and ASMTP specifically for technical professionals. This is a dedicated fast-track channel for skilled technical workers sponsored by employers.

---

### 5. Quality Migrant Admission Scheme (QMAS) 優秀人才入境計劃

Points-based scheme. No job offer required, no sector restriction. Two assessment routes:

**General Points Test (GPT)** — scored across six aspects: Age, Academic Qualifications, Language Proficiency, Work Experience, Annual Income, and Business Ownership.

**Achievement-based Points Test (APT)** — for individuals with outstanding achievements such as Olympic medals, Nobel Prize, or major national/international awards.

---

### 6. Technology Talent Admission Scheme (TechTAS) 科技人才入境計劃

Fast-track scheme for tech R&D professionals sponsored by eligible companies. Key requirements: the employing company must hold a valid quota approved by the Innovation and Technology Commission (ITC); the applicant must be principally engaged in R&D in areas such as AI, biotechnology, cybersecurity, fintech, green technology, quantum technology, robotics, and others; the applicant must hold a STEM degree from a well-recognised university; and remuneration must not be below the prevailing market level. Stay pattern: **3+3+2 years.**

---

### 7. Immigration Arrangements for Non-local Graduates (IANG) 非本地畢業生留港計劃

For graduates who obtained an undergraduate or higher qualification in a full-time locally-accredited programme in Hong Kong. Recent graduates are not required to have a job offer upon entry — they receive an initial stay of 2 years to seek employment. Non-recent graduates must have a job at a level appropriate for degree holders, remunerated at the market rate. The scheme has been expanded to include graduates from GBA campuses of Hong Kong universities. Stay pattern: 2+3+3 years.

---

### 8. Admission Scheme for the Second Generation of Chinese Hong Kong Permanent Residents (ASSG) 第二代計劃

For overseas-born second-generation Chinese Hong Kong permanent residents. No sector restriction, no job offer required upon entry. Key requirements: aged 18–40, born overseas; at least one parent holding a valid Hong Kong permanent identity card who was a Chinese national settled overseas at the time of the applicant's birth; good education background or proven professional experience; and proficiency in written and spoken Chinese (Putonghua or Cantonese) or English. Stay pattern: 2+3+3 years.

---

### 9. Vocational Professionals Admission Scheme (VPAS) 🆕 *(Coming mid-2026)*

A new scheme for non-local students enrolled in designated full-time Vocational Training Council Higher Diploma programmes. Applications will open upon the graduation of the first batch of eligible students in mid-2026.

## TTPS vs QMAS — Key Differences

### Fundamental Approach

**TTPS** seeks to attract top talents with rich work experience and good academic qualifications, targeting high-income professionals and graduates of the world's top universities.

**QMAS** is designed for highly skilled or talented individuals who have not yet secured a job offer, with no sector restriction — assessed through a points-based system.

---

### 1. Eligibility Basis

**TTPS (Category B)** is straightforward and binary: you must hold a degree from one of the eligible universities (drawn from top global and Mainland rankings), with at least 3 years of work experience over the past 5 years. Either you qualify or you don't — there's no scoring.

**QMAS** is competitive and points-based. The General Points Test assesses you across six aspects: Age, Academic Qualifications, Language Proficiency, Work Experience, Annual Income, and Business Ownership. Your university does not need to be on any approved list, but you need to score competitively against other applicants in the selection pool.

---

### 2. University Requirement

This is one of the sharpest differences:

- **TTPS** — your degree *must* be from one of the **198 eligible universities** on the aggregate list (top global rankings + top Mainland universities + specialist lists for hospitality and art & design). A degree from a good but unlisted university will disqualify you entirely.
- **QMAS** — no such restriction. Any good academic qualification can earn you points, and broader achievements and experience can compensate.

---

### 3. Visa Validity on Entry

TTPS Category B grants an initial stay of **24 months** on time limitation only, with no conditions of stay — meaning you are free to work, change jobs, or set up a business without needing further approval during that period.

QMAS also grants an initial stay of **24 months** under similar conditions, so the two are equivalent here.

---

### 4. Selection Process & Certainty

- **TTPS** — if you meet the criteria, approval is largely objective and predictable. Processing normally takes about **4 weeks**.
- **QMAS** — involves a competitive selection exercise. You are scored and ranked against other applicants in a quota-based pool. Approval is less predictable and can take considerably longer.

---

### 5. Stay Extension Pattern

Both follow a **3+3+2 year** pattern on extension (after the initial stay), and both offer a **top-tier employment stream** — if after at least 2 years of stay you have an assessable salaries tax income of HKD 2 million or more, you may apply for a **6-year extension** in one go.

---

|---|---|---|
| Degree from top-ranked uni? | **Required** | Not required |
| Job offer needed? | No | No |
| Selection method | Objective criteria | Competitive points scoring |
| Predictability | High | Lower |
| Initial visa | 24 months | 24 months |
| Processing | ~4 weeks | Longer / quota-based |
| Sector restriction | None | None |

---

### Bottom Line

If your degree is from one of the 198 eligible universities, **TTPS Category B is generally the faster and more straightforward route** — you either qualify or you don't, and there's no competition with other applicants. QMAS is the better option if your university isn't on the approved list, or if your broader profile (income, language, business ownership) gives you a strong points score that compensates for a weaker academic credential.

### TTPS Quota — Category B vs Category C

**Category B — No quota.** Degree graduates of the eligible universities with at least 3 years of work experience over the past 5 years are assessed purely on whether they meet the criteria. There is no annual quota limiting approvals.

**Category C — Quota applies.** Degree graduates of the eligible universities in the past 5 years with *less than* 3 years of work experience are subject to an annual quota, allotted on a **first-come, first-served basis**.

---

### What This Means Practically

For someone with **3+ years of experience**, this is actually good news — they fall squarely under **Category B**, which has **no quota** and no race against other applicants for limited slots. As long as the eligibility criteria are met, the application is assessed on its own merits.

Category C, by contrast, carries timing risk — if you apply late in the year after the annual quota is exhausted, your application will not be approved regardless of how strong your profile is.
