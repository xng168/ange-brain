### 🤖 **Claude**

## Australian Tax Residency & Foreign Income

As an Australian tax resident, Australia taxes you on your **worldwide income** — meaning all income you earn, regardless of where in the world it comes from.

### The Core Principle

The ATO (Australian Taxation Office) requires you to declare all foreign income in your Australian tax return, including:

- **Employment income** earned while working overseas
- **Business income** from foreign operations
- **Investment income** — dividends, interest, and rent from foreign assets
- **Capital gains** on foreign assets
- **Pension or annuity** payments from overseas

---

### How It's Taxed

Foreign income is converted to AUD and added to your other Australian income, then taxed at your **marginal tax rate** using the standard Australian income tax brackets (up to 45% for income over $180,000, plus the 2% Medicare Levy).

---

### Avoiding Double Taxation

The good news is Australia has mechanisms to prevent you from being taxed twice on the same income:

**1. Foreign Income Tax Offset (FITO)**
If you've already paid tax on that income in another country, you can claim a foreign income tax offset against your Australian tax liability. The offset is generally capped at the Australian tax payable on that foreign income.

**2. Double Tax Agreements (DTAs)**
Australia has DTAs with over 40 countries (including the US, UK, Japan, China, Germany). These treaties determine which country has the primary taxing right and can reduce or eliminate withholding taxes on certain income types.

---

### Special Cases Worth Noting

| Income Type | Key Consideration |
|---|---|
| **Foreign employment income** | May be exempt if you paid foreign tax and worked for a foreign employer for 91+ continuous days |
| **Foreign dividends** | Subject to FITO; franking credits generally don't apply |
| **Capital gains on foreign property** | CGT applies; main residence exemption may be limited |
| **Controlled Foreign Companies (CFCs)** | Attributable income rules may apply if you control a foreign company |
| **Foreign superannuation** | Complex rules; timing of receipt matters significantly |

---

### Practical Steps

1. **Keep records** of all foreign income and any foreign tax paid (tax assessments, payment receipts)
2. **Convert to AUD** using the ATO's published exchange rates or the rate at the time of transaction
3. **Declare everything** on your tax return — the ATO receives data from foreign tax authorities under automatic exchange of information agreements
4. **Consider a tax adviser** if your situation is complex (e.g., foreign property, share options, or business interests)

---

## Non-Residents & Foreign Income Sent to an Australian Bank

This is a common misconception worth clearing up:

### The Core Principle

**Where money is banked is irrelevant for Australian tax purposes.** The ATO taxes income based on its *source*, not where it's deposited. Simply sending foreign-sourced income to an Australian bank account does **not** make it taxable in Australia.

---

### What Non-Residents Are Taxed On

As a non-resident, Australia only taxes you on **Australian-sourced income**, which includes:

| Income Type | Taxed in Australia? |
|---|---|
| Income earned from work performed in Australia | ✅ Yes |
| Rent from Australian property | ✅ Yes |
| Capital gains on Australian property | ✅ Yes |
| Australian bank interest | ✅ Yes (withholding tax) |
| Australian dividends | ✅ Yes (withholding tax) |
| **Foreign income deposited into an Australian bank** | ❌ No |
| **Foreign salary earned and sourced overseas** | ❌ No |
| **Foreign investment income** | ❌ No |

---

### The Australian Bank Interest Nuance

There is one important exception — the **interest earned on your Australian bank account** is Australian-sourced income, and therefore taxable in Australia, even as a non-resident. This is typically collected via **withholding tax**:

- Non-resident withholding tax on interest is generally **10%**
- The bank automatically deducts this — you usually don't need to lodge a return just for this
- DTAs with certain countries may reduce this rate

---

### Non-Resident Tax Rates

If you do have Australian-sourced income, note that non-residents:
- Are taxed at **32.5% from the first dollar** (no tax-free threshold)
- Are **not** subject to the Medicare Levy
- Are taxed up to 45% for income over $180,000

---

### Key Risks to Be Aware Of

**1. Residency classification is not always straightforward**
The ATO uses a facts-and-circumstances test. Factors like maintaining an Australian home, family ties, and frequency of visits can mean the ATO *disputes* your non-resident status.

**2. The "temporary resident" category**
If you hold a temporary visa and are a temporary resident for tax purposes, slightly different rules apply — most foreign income is still exempt, but there are nuances around capital gains.

**3. CGT on Taxable Australian Property (TAP)**
Even as a non-resident, you remain liable for capital gains tax on Australian real property and certain other Australian assets.

---

### Bottom Line

> Depositing foreign income into an Australian bank **does not trigger Australian tax**. What matters is where the income was *earned and sourced*, not where it lands. The only amount taxable is the **interest that account earns** in Australia.
