### 🤖 **Claude**

Here are 20 engaging interview prompts for your sunglasses content:

**Style & Identity**
1. *"If your sunglasses could talk, what would they say about you?"*
2. *"Do you think the sunglasses someone wears says something about their personality?"*
3. *"What's the most iconic pair of sunglasses in history — and why?"*

**Fun & Playful**
4. *"Aviators, wayfarers, or cat-eyes — which one screams the most confidence?"*
5. *"Have you ever judged someone (or been judged) purely based on their sunglasses?"*
6. *"What celebrity's sunglasses would you steal if you could?"*
7. *"If sunglasses were banned tomorrow, how would your life change?"*
8. *"Would you ever spend $500+ on a pair of sunglasses? What's your limit?"*

**Storytelling Gold**
9. *"What's the story behind the sunglasses you're wearing right now?"*
10. *"Have you ever lost a pair of sunglasses you truly loved? Tell me about them."*
11. *"What's the most random place you've ever found or bought a pair of sunnies?"*
12. *"Has a pair of sunglasses ever given you a confidence boost? Walk me through it."*

**Opinion & Debate**
13. *"Is it ever NOT okay to wear sunglasses indoors?"*
14. *"Cheap $10 sunnies vs. designer — can you actually tell the difference?"*
15. *"Do men style sunglasses differently than women, or is it all the same?"*
16. *"Are oversized sunglasses a power move or a fashion crime?"*

**Nostalgic & Emotional**
17. *"What's your earliest sunglasses memory — like the first pair you ever loved?"*
18. *"Did anyone in your life — a parent, a friend — influence how you wear sunglasses?"*
19. *"If you could design your perfect pair from scratch, what would they look like?"*

**The Big Closer**
20. *"In one word — what do sunglasses mean to you?"*

---

A few tips for the street interviews: lead with the lighter, playful questions to warm people up, and save the storytelling ones (like #9, #10, #12) for people who seem chatty — those tend to produce the most shareable, emotional moments. Question #20 is a perfect closer that gives you great short-form clip material.

### 🤖 **Claude**

Here are some engaging prompts and a conversation structure for your designer vs. your brand comparison content:

---

## 🎬 The Setup (Hook the Viewer First)
Open with something like:
> *"I'm going to show two pairs of sunglasses to strangers — one costs $400, one costs $60. Let's see if anyone can tell the difference."*

This frames the entire video as a challenge/experiment, which is instantly watchable.

---

## 🔍 Round 1 — The Blind Touch & Feel Test
Hand both pairs to the stranger **without revealing prices or brands.**

- *"Just hold both pairs — which one feels more premium to you?"*
- *"Which one do you think is better quality, just from touching them?"*
- *"Which pair would you trust more to last?"*

**Why it works:** The reaction when they pick yours is pure gold content.

---

## 👓 Round 2 — The Wear Test
Ask them to put both pairs on (one at a time).

- *"Which one feels more comfortable on your face?"*
- *"Which one do you think looks better on you — be honest!"*
- *"If you were heading to the beach right now, which pair are you grabbing?"*

---

## 💰 Round 3 — The Price Reveal
This is your **money moment.** Reveal the prices after they've already given their opinion.

- *"What if I told you one of these is $400 and one is $60 — does that change anything for you?"*
- *"Could you guess which one is which just by looking?"*
- *"Now that you know the prices — do you still stand by your pick?"*

**Capture the reaction in full.** The surprise, the laugh, the disbelief — that's your viral moment.

---

## 🧠 Round 4 — The Opinion Deep Dive
Once the reveal lands, dig into their thoughts:

- *"Do you think people pay for the quality, or just the logo?"*
- *"Would you feel embarrassed telling friends you paid $60 instead of $400?"*
- *"What's the real difference between luxury and affordable — is it the product or the story?"*
- *"If nobody could see the brand name, would designer even matter to you?"*

---

## ❤️ Round 5 — The Close
End each interview on something memorable:

- *"If these were sitting side by side in a store with no price tags — which one sells itself?"*
- *"Knowing what you know now, which pair would you actually buy?"*
- *"One word to describe the cheaper pair after holding both — go!"*

---

## 📐 Suggested Video Structure

| Segment | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Hook + Setup | 5–10 sec | Grab attention |
| Blind test reactions | 20–30 sec | Build suspense |
| Price reveal reaction | 10–15 sec | Viral moment |
| Opinion soundbite | 15–20 sec | Credibility + relatability |
| Closing one-liner | 5 sec | Punchline/CTA |

---

## 💡 Pro Tips
- **Stack 3–4 stranger interviews** in one video so viewers see multiple people reaching the same conclusion — it feels like proof, not a fluke.
- **Let silence do the work** after the price reveal — don't cut away too fast.
- End with a caption or on-screen text like *"Same quality. Different price tag. You decide."* to plant your brand message without being salesy.
