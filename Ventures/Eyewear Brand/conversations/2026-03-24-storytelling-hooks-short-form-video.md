## 🎬 VIDEO STRUCTURE FORMULA

For each video, use this proven short-form structure:

```
0-2s   → HOOK     (the line that stops the scroll)
2-8s   → TENSION  (deepen the emotion or stakes)
8-25s  → STORY    (the real, specific, human moment)
25-35s → TURN     (the insight, decision, or revelation)
35-45s → CTA      (follow the journey / comment your thoughts)
```

---

## 💡 Tone Tips

- **Speak to ONE person**, not an audience — "you" not "you guys"
- **Specific details beat vague emotion** — "I had £2,300 left" hits harder than "I had no money"
- **Silence and pauses** are as powerful as words in these formats
- **No music in the first 3 seconds** — let your voice be the hook
- Film some content **in the moment**, not just recreated — authenticity is the product

## 📱 HOW TO FILM IT

Since this is a one-person documentary, keep it simple but intentional:

**Kit**
- iPhone on a small gimbal for smooth walking shots
- A small tripod for sit-down talking head moments
- Natural light always over artificial — especially golden hour

**Shot Types to Always Get**
- **The walk** — you walking toward camera or away, in new environments
- **The hands** — handling prototypes, pointing at details, writing notes
- **The face** — unguarded reactions, first impressions, emotional moments
- **The place** — wide establishing shots of China that make the world feel real

**Rule of thumb** — *film first, decide later.* You won't know which moments matter until you're editing.

### 🧑 **Angela**

which is a better hook "i heard this somewhere", "I heard this recently", "this is what I heard recently" which out of the three?

---

### 🤖 **Claude**

**"I heard this recently"** — no question.

Here's why:

- **"I heard this somewhere"** — "somewhere" sounds vague and unsure, slightly undermines the credibility of what you're about to say
- **"I heard this recently"** — clean, curious, creates immediacy. "Recently" makes it feel fresh and relevant
- **"This is what I heard recently"** — too long and loses the punchy energy you want in a hook, the "this is what" slows it down before you've even started

**"I heard this recently"** then a pause — lets the viewer lean in wondering *what* you heard. Simple and effective. ✅
