## What is a Trademark?

A trademark protects brand identifiers — names, logos, slogans — that distinguish your goods/services from others. It gives you exclusive legal rights to use that mark in commerce within specific categories.

---

## Before You File: Key Considerations

**1. Trademark Search (Do This First)**
Before buying your domain or filing anything, search existing trademarks:
- **USPTO TESS database** (US): tess2.uspto.gov
- **EUIPO** (Europe), **WIPO** (international)
- Also search Google, social media, and common law uses — unregistered marks can still have legal protection

**2. Distinctiveness Matters**
Trademarks are ranked by strength:
- ✅ **Fanciful** (invented words, e.g. *Kodak*) — strongest protection
- ✅ **Arbitrary** (real word, unrelated meaning, e.g. *Apple* for computers)
- ⚠️ **Suggestive** (hints at product, e.g. *Netflix*) — protectable but weaker
- ❌ **Descriptive** (describes the product, e.g. "ColdBrew Coffee") — very hard to protect
- ❌ **Generic** — not protectable at all

**3. Classes of Goods/Services**
Trademarks are registered by industry class (there are 45 Nice Classification classes). You only get protection in the classes you register for. Choose the right ones for your business.

**4. Domain Name vs. Trademark — They Are NOT the Same**
Owning a domain does **not** give you trademark rights, and vice versa. However, having a matching domain strengthens your brand position. If someone else owns the trademark for your chosen name in your industry, they could force you to relinquish the domain later.

---

## The Trademark Registration Process (US-focused)

**Step 1: Conduct a Thorough Search**
Use USPTO's TESS, hire a trademark attorney, or use services like Trademarkia. Look for phonetically similar names too, not just exact matches.

**Step 2: Determine the Basis for Filing**
- **Use in Commerce** — already using the mark in business
- **Intent to Use (ITU)** — not yet in use but you plan to; this is common when you're at the domain-buying stage

**Step 3: File Your Application (USPTO)**
- File at **USPTO.gov** online via TEAS (Trademark Electronic Application System)
- Costs: ~$250–$350 per class (TEAS Plus vs. Standard)
- You'll need: the mark, goods/services description, filing basis, and a specimen (if already in use)

**Step 4: USPTO Examination (~3–6 months)**
A USPTO examiner reviews your application. They may issue an **Office Action** (a refusal or request for clarification) that you must respond to.

**Step 5: Publication for Opposition (30 days)**
If approved, the mark is published in the Official Gazette. Third parties can oppose within 30 days.

**Step 6: Registration or Notice of Allowance**
- If no opposition: **Registration certificate** issued (if "use in commerce")
- If ITU basis: you receive a **Notice of Allowance** and must file a Statement of Use within 6 months (extendable up to 3 years)

**Total timeline: typically 12–18 months**

---

## Domain Name + Trademark Strategy

| Action | Timing |
|---|---|
| Check trademark databases | Before buying domain |
| Buy your domain | As soon as name is cleared |
| File ITU trademark application | Immediately or shortly after |
| Launch brand/website | While application is pending |
| Submit Statement of Use | Once live in commerce |

**Key tips:**
- Buy the domain **before** filing, since filing is public and could tip off squatters
- Consider buying common variations (.net, .co, common misspellings)
- ™ can be used immediately upon filing; ® only after registration

---

## When to Hire an Attorney

Strongly recommended if:
- Your business will operate in multiple countries
- The name is in a crowded trademark space
- You receive an Office Action from the USPTO
- You're investing significantly in the brand

Services like **LegalZoom**, **Trademarkia**, or **Gerben Law** offer affordable flat-fee filing if budget is a concern.

---

**Bottom line:** Do your trademark search *before* buying the domain and filing. The ITU filing process is your friend — it lets you secure trademark priority even before launch.
