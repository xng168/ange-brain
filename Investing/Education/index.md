# Education

Core concepts, growing as you learn. Ask me anytime to expand any section or explain something in more depth.

## The basics
- **Share/stock:** a unit of ownership in a company. Price moves based on supply/demand, driven by earnings, growth expectations, news, and broader market sentiment.
- **Market cap:** share price × number of shares outstanding — the market's valuation of the whole company.
- **Dividend:** a portion of profit paid out to shareholders, usually per-share, often quarterly or semi-annually (varies by market).

## Fundamental analysis (is the company good value?)
- **P/E ratio (Price/Earnings):** price per share ÷ earnings per share. Higher = market expects more future growth (or the stock is overpriced). Compare within the same industry, not across industries.
- **P/B ratio (Price/Book):** price per share ÷ book value per share. Useful for asset-heavy businesses (banks, resources).
- **Debt/Equity:** how much debt a company carries relative to shareholder equity. Higher = more financial risk, but not automatically bad (context-dependent).
- **Dividend yield:** annual dividend ÷ share price. Higher isn't automatically better — very high yields can signal a struggling company (the price has fallen more than the dividend has been cut yet).

## Technical analysis (when might be a good time to buy/sell?)
- **Moving averages:** smoothed average price over N days — used to spot trend direction.
- **Support/resistance:** price levels where a stock has historically struggled to fall below (support) or rise above (resistance).
- **Volume:** how many shares traded — a price move on high volume is more meaningful than the same move on low volume.

## Risk management (arguably more important than picking winners)
- **Position sizing:** never put so much into one stock that a single bad outcome derails you.
- **Diversification:** spreading risk across sectors/markets so no single event wipes you out.
- **Have a thesis before you buy:** write down *why* you're buying (see [[Investing/Journal]]) — makes it much easier to know when to sell, and to learn from outcomes either way.

## Order types (practical mechanics)
- **Market order:** buy/sell immediately at the best available price.
- **Limit order:** buy/sell only at a specific price or better — you control price, not timing.
- **Stop-loss:** an order that triggers a sale if the price falls to a set level, to cap downside.

## A note on scope
This system is for **research, education, and decision support** — not automated trading. No trades are ever executed on your behalf.
