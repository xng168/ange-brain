Here's a comprehensive list of apps and websites focused on bite-sized, accessible financial literacy:

---

**🎮 Gamified / Bite-Sized Learning Apps**

**Zogo** — Breaks down complicated financial topics into fun bite-sized modules, rewarding users with points redeemable for real-life rewards. Free to use.

**Money Masters (MNYMSTRS)** — Turns five-minute lessons into money skills, with quizzes, leaderboards, and monthly gift cards for top scorers. Topics range from budgeting to crypto.

**Finny (askfinny.com)** — A free, game-based personal finance education app described as "Duolingo for personal finance," with bubble-style levels that summarize key points after completion.

**Your Juno** — Aimed at women, it offers individual courses, quizzes, and action-oriented lists alongside a community to network. The team describes it as "Duolingo meets Masterclass."

**KidVestors** — Covers earning, spending, saving, and investing for ages 8–18, with real cash and stock rewards. Available in 50+ languages.

**Greenlight** — A banking and financial education app for kids and teens with debit cards, parental controls, savings incentives, and investing tools.

---

**🌐 Free Learning Platforms & Websites**

**Khan Academy** — Covers budgeting, saving, investing, and credit through video lessons paired with interactive exercises and quizzes, with gamification elements like badges and points — all completely free.

**Banzai (banzai.org)** — Offers 15+ interactive games and 40+ worksheets covering topics like budgeting, debt, and home buying. Available in 20 languages and free through sponsorships from local banks and credit unions.

**Intuit for Education** — Provides free curriculum for high school and college students using real-world simulations, games, and tools like QuickBooks, including a tax simulation students can complete in under an hour.

**MoneySkill (AFSA Education Foundation)** — A free, customizable online personal finance program covering 37 money topics, designed especially for students from middle school through college.

**Investopedia** — A widely used reference site with a full education section covering everything from beginner definitions to advanced investing concepts, with explanations on how financial elements interact with each other.

**AARP Financial Literacy Resource Center** — Tailored for older adults, it addresses retirement planning, healthcare costs, and managing fixed incomes.

---

**📱 Investment-Focused Learning**

**Acorns** — Combines micro-investing with built-in educational content, teaching users about investing passively as they use the app.

**Robinhood Learn** — Offers a library of plain-English explainer articles on stocks, ETFs, options, and crypto alongside its trading platform.

**Public.com** — Blends a social investing community with educational content around market trends and asset classes.

---

**🧒 For Younger Audiences Specifically**

**Bankaroo** — A virtual bank for kids to practice managing allowances and savings goals.

**PiggyBot** — Teaches young children the basics of saving, spending, and sharing.

---

The space is growing fast — most of these lean into short lessons, gamification, and rewards to keep users engaged. The best pick depends on your age group and whether you want pure learning or something paired with actual financial tools.
