# TSM — Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co. (NYSE ADR)

## Why it's on the radar
Dominant leading-edge foundry (2nm/3nm) — sole supplier for Nvidia, Apple, and AMD's most advanced chips. A "picks-and-shovels" way to get exposure to the AI buildout without betting on which AI product/company wins.

- Market cap: ~$2.25T
- P/E (trailing): 37.7

Note: yfinance returned a P/B of 98x, which looks like a data glitch (TSM's historical P/B has run 6–12x) — don't trust that field, re-check with another source before acting on it.

## Thesis
- Leading-edge process dominance = pricing power, hard to displace as sole advanced-node supplier
- Low leverage, consistently profitable despite being capital-intensive
- AI/GPU demand is a multi-year tailwind, not a one-quarter blip

## Risks to watch
- Taiwan/China geopolitical risk — single point of failure, doesn't show up in any ratio
- Customer concentration (Nvidia, Apple loom large in revenue mix)
- Valuation: 37x trailing earnings near 52-week highs — a lot of AI growth already priced in
- Heavy, cyclical capex (Arizona, Japan fab buildout) pressures margins if AI demand softens

## Watching for
- Whether the recent -3.9% 5-day pullback is noise or the start of AI-capex-fatigue sentiment
- Quarterly earnings for signs of demand softening from major customers
