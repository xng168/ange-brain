## Chinese Knots (中国结)

Chinese knots are a form of traditional folk art with a history stretching back thousands of years. Originally used for practical purposes — fastening clothing, recording information, and binding objects — they evolved into richly symbolic decorative art forms, especially during the Tang and Song dynasties.

The craft is characterized by using a single length of cord, interlaced and folded symmetrically, to create a finished knot that looks the same on both sides. The most traditional color is **red**, symbolizing good luck, prosperity, and joy — though gold, green, and other auspicious colors are also common.

### General Symbolism

Chinese knots as a whole carry deep cultural meaning:
- **Continuity and eternity** — the unbroken cord represents an unending flow of good fortune
- **Unity and connection** — knots are exchanged between friends and lovers as tokens of lasting bonds
- **Protection** — hung in homes and worn as pendants to ward off evil

## The Clover Leaf Knot (酢浆草结)

The **clover leaf knot** (literally "wood sorrel grass knot") is one of the most beloved and commonly used knots in Chinese knotting. Its name comes from its resemblance to the three-lobed clover or wood sorrel plant.

### Symbolism
- **Good luck** — the clover is universally associated with fortune across many cultures, and in Chinese tradition this is amplified by the auspicious shape
- **The number three** — the three lobes can represent the **Three Abundances** (福 fú, 禄 lù, 寿 shòu) — blessing, prosperity, and longevity
- **Harmony** — the balanced, symmetrical petals suggest equilibrium in life

### Role in Chinese Knotting
The clover leaf knot is considered a **foundational knot** — it's one of the first techniques beginners learn, and it also serves as a decorative connector or accent piece in larger, more complex knotwork compositions. You'll often see it used as:
- A finishing flourish at the ends of tassels
- A connecting element between larger knots in hanging decorations
- A standalone pendant or charm

### Variations
An **upgraded or double clover leaf knot** adds extra loops to create a fuller, more elaborate flower-like shape, sometimes with four or more lobes, each additional lobe compounding the sense of luck and abundance.

---

Chinese knots like the clover leaf are still widely made today for **Lunar New Year decorations**, wedding gifts, temple offerings, and personal jewelry — a living tradition linking modern life to ancient craft.
