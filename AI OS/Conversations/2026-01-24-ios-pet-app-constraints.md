# iOS Pet App: Roaming vs. Platform Constraints

## Summary

- **iOS limitation identified:** App-overlay graphics (roaming pet) blocked by system sandbox; investigated workarounds to preserve the companion feeling within Apple's constraints.

- **5 viable iOS mechanics ranked:** Pet-text notifications (styled as communication messages with avatar), lock-screen + home-screen widget, Dynamic Island live activity during focus sessions, customizable Screen Time block screens, and sound/badge touches.

- **Notification + widget stack validated:** Pet checks in via iOS-native communication notifications (styled to look like text messages from the pet, not system alerts) paired with a living widget showing mood/energy based on screen time—creates a "glanceable, message-based" pet experience (not roaming, but emotionally coherent).

- **iOS Path A vs. Path B trade-off:** Simpler goal/schedule-based triggers ship faster without Apple entitlements; Screen Time–triggered nudges (with custom block screen at the moment of temptation) are more "smart" but require FamilyControls approval and an extension.

- **Strategic decision needed:** iOS as v1 or v2 port? Desktop roaming proves the core concept stronger; iOS works as a complementary mobile experience but trades ambient presence for glanceability.

- **Next:** Document iOS findings in venture note, then scope the desktop-first MVP and confirm platform priorities.
