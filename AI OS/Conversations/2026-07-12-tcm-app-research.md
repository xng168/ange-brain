# TCM App: Market Research & Launch Planning

## Session Summary

**What was done:**
- Formulated "TCM App," a working name for a MyFitnessPal-style food/symptom tracker layered with Traditional Chinese Medicine properties (thermal nature, organ affinity, seasonal recommendations)
- Completed market research: $18–20B personalized nutrition market; Ayurveda diet apps (AyuRythm, Prana, Dosha Diet) proving the "traditional medicine + personalized app" category is fundable at 12.7% CAGR
- Identified whitespace: no polished app combines daily food logging + TCM property tagging + symptom/season recommendations; existing TCM tools are static lead-gen quizzes, not trackers
- Mapped data sourcing strategy using public-domain classical texts (*Compendium of Materia Medica*, *Huangdi Neijing*), Pitchford's *Healing with Whole Foods*, academic pharmacology databases (TCMSP, ETCM, TCMID), nutrient-correlation statistical bridging method, and licensed practitioner review layer
- Documented venture in Obsidian vault (Ventures/TCM App/index.md) + indexed in main Ventures list

**Key decisions:**
- Target model: freemium mobile app (subscription for personalized recommendations)
- Data authority: classical texts as primary source + human review by licensed TCM practitioners for credibility & liability protection
- Positioning: "ancient wisdom meets modern tracking" (same narrative lane as Meridian venture)
- Identified audience tension: Chinese-diaspora users (want accuracy, will judge hard) vs. Western wellness audience (want quick/vibes content) — this cascades into everything downstream

**What's next:**
Validate demand cheaply *before* building: post TCM wellness content on existing channels (Ange.beee) — test engagement/saves/comments on "is ginger hot/cold, what to eat for flu" style videos. That's a free signal that tells you which audience actually responds. Five key follow-ups identified:
1. **Validate demand via content** (fastest, uses existing platform)
2. **Pick primary audience & geography** (dictates data sources, tone, distribution, regulatory focus)
3. **Check health-claim regulation** (App Store/Play Store policies + target-market regulator, e.g. TGA for Australia)
4. **Benchmark Ayurveda monetization** (actual ARPU & conversion rates, not just growth metrics)
5. **Find sharper wedge** (postpartum confinement foods, cycle-based eating, seasonal eating for diaspora — narrower first audience easier to market than "all of TCM nutrition")

*Start with #1; its results should inform how you answer #2.*
