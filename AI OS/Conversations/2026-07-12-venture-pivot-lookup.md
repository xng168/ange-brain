# Venture Pivot: Tracker → Lookup/Repository Model

- **Major pivot executed**: Reframed the product from an assumption of logging/tracking (implicit dependency on user habit formation) to a lookup/repository model — free ingredient database with constitution-quiz personalization as the differentiation layer. Product ladder now: free search → quiz → tracking only if demand validates it later.

- **MVP scope redefined**: Searchable ingredient database, symptom/goal-based browse, seasonal discovery content, constitution quiz, and save/favorites. Logging, journaling, and correlation tracking explicitly moved from assumed backlog to "future phases — only if evidence emerges."

- **Open gaps reprioritized**: Key risks shift from product build to go-to-market: (1) differentiation from free web search (search itself is commodity), (2) monetization rethink needed (reference tools lack the engagement/streak dynamics of trackers), (3) retention strategy moves from logging-streaks to content freshness and search discovery.

- **Data strategy elevated**: Because the ingredient database *is* the product (not a feature behind tracking), database quality and curation now drive competitive advantage. Content sourcing strategy remains the same.

- **Market research carried over unchanged**: Competitor landscape, candidate wedges (TCM dampness/gut health, cycle syncing, postpartum confinement), and data sources all remain intact — none depended on the tracker assumption.

- **Next step**: TikTok content test should validate which wedge (dampness/bloating vs. cycle syncing vs. postpartum) drives real demand before committing to Super Prompt + build roadmap.
